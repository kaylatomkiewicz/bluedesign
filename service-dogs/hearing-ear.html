Hearing Ear Dog

For people with hearing impairment, the absence of sound can be life-threatening. Hearing Ear Dogs learn to respond to verbal and hand signals and are taught to work for toys and affection. Hearing Ear Dogs are trained to alert their owners to a variety of different sounds. When the dog hears any of the noises they are trained to recognize, they get their owners attention and lead them to the sound.

Hearing Ear Dogs can be a bridge providing more opportunities for their owners to socialize while out and about in a “hearing” world. People with hearing impairments who use a Hearing Ear Dog report that they feel better equipped to function in the outside world.

Hearing Aid Dogs are trained to work in any situation. Some of the sounds these dogs can be trained to respond to include (but are not limited to):

  • Someone knocking at the door,
  • Doorbells,
  • Alarm clocks,
  • Telephones,
  • Smoke detectors,
  • Someone calling their owner’s name,
  • Police and emergency vehicle sirens.
